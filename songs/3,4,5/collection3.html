music collection 3: november sixth 2020 to december thirtieth 2020

me move crab neckties

This is a background track i made for a documentary for my science class, about the history and effects of climate change.
It does use four samples, from an old sample cd called "Zero-G Datafile 1" and "Zero-G Datafile 2". One of them is a drumloop at the end, one of them is a record scratch, and another one is a sample of a fax machine, i think? I'm not quite sure.

the snare drum at the beginning (Roland TR-909) is from a youtuber called "Bouncy Glow's Music Room" from this link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KenvUibY7uc (update: it comes from a synthesizer called the Korg T3.)

also, the weird title is an anagram for "time never comes back"

to you

second piece i made for the documentary in science class. I had to mute this a ton in the actual video because of how little it is "background music".

Samples used
chord from "X-Static Goldmine 1"
choir from "Zero-G Datafile 2"
record scratch from “Zero-G Datafile 1”
cymbal at the end from sonic cd past pcm channel

the old title was an anagram for something, but i didn't write down what it was :(

electric cell zone act 1

song i made in the style of sonic r / sonic cd. all instruments used were stock garageband except for the bass, which was a combination of an organ from a synthesizer and one from garageband.

electric cell zone comes from the jumpiness of the song

electric cell zone act 2

act 2 of electric cell zone. the analog chords near the second part are from an app called "animoog".
the sound effect as a transition was a pitched down cymbal overlayed with a bubble wrap pop sound.

help (without phaser)

this song is called help 4 because it's the fourth revision of a song i came up with in the middle of the night, hence the name "help".
the original version had an phaser effect over the whole thing, but i didn't really like it so i removed it.
the guitar playing one note throughout the whole song is inspired by "Voyager" by Daft Punk.

the four samples (at the beginning, and through the song) are from a song "Wake Me, Shake Me" on a cd called "harmony road toddler tunes" from a music class "harmony road" when i was three years old or so.
